AI CV Builder Methodology — How to Transform Questionnaire Data into a Published Digital Twin Portfolio
This document explains the exact methodology, data structures, component architecture, system prompt design, and chatbot tuning used to build production-quality AI CVs. Follow this precisely when generating an AI CV from customer questionnaire responses.

PART 1: DATA ARCHITECTURE — How Questionnaire Data Maps to the CV
The portfolio is driven by ONE central data object (RESUME_DATA in client/src/lib/data.ts). The questionnaire answers must be transformed into this exact structure:
export const RESUME_DATA = {
 name: "Full Name",
 hero: {
   title: "Full Name",
   subtitle: "Title 1 • Title 2 • Title 3",
   // IMPORTANT: Description is NOT a bullet list. It's 2 paragraphs:
   // Paragraph 1: One-line positioning ("I consult, design and scale...")
   // Paragraph 2: A specific proof story — their most impressive recent role, with concrete outcomes
   description: "I [verb] [what they do] for [who].\n\nAt [Most Recent Company], I [specific achievement with metrics]. This shifted [what] from [before state] to [after state] that [measurable outcome].",
   meta: {
     labelDigitalPortfolio: "Digital Twin Interface (aka Ai Cv)",
     currentFocusLabel: "Current Focus",
     currentFocusValue: "[What they're focused on now]",
     locationLabel: "Location",
     locationValue: "[City (Country)]"
   },
   cta: {
     primary: { label: "Talk to My AI Twin", action: "scroll_or_open_twin_interface" },
     secondary: { label: "Watch 60-Second Overview", action: "play_intro_video" }
   }
 },
 videoSection: {
   title: "Hear Me",
   description: "A 60 second self introduction",
 },
 twinInterface: {
   headerTitle: "Twin Interface",

   // 1. Speak in first person as the person
   // 2. Name their specific domains
   // 3. Invite specific question types
   introMessage: "Hello, I'm [Name]'s AI Twin. I think in [their mental models]. Ask me about [domain 1], [domain 2], [domain 3], or how I handle [challenge type].",

   suggestedQuestions: [

   ]
 },
 // CAREER TRAJECTORY — Maps to timeline in Bento Grid
 roles: [
   {
     company: "Company Name",

     period: "YYYY – YYYY",

TRANSFORMATION RULES (Questionnaire → RESUME_DATA):
hero.subtitle: Take their role title and split into 3 facets separated by " • ". Don't just copy the title — reframe it into positioning language.
hero.description: NEVER just paste their career summary. Write 2 paragraphs: line 1 = positioning statement, line 2 = their most impressive specific story with metrics.
stats: Extract ALL numbers and metrics from their stories. Every % improvement, dollar amount, team size, and time reduction becomes a stat card.
problemFit.useCases: Rewrite their skills as the BUYER'S problem. Not "I can do X" but "You need X and your current approach isn't working."
suggestedQuestions: Write questions that a HIRING MANAGER would ask, not generic questions. They should map to the CAR stories in the knowledge base.

PART 2: PAGE LAYOUT — The Exact Section Flow

Section A: Introduction (hero + video/photo)
 ├── Left: Name, subtitle, description, CTA buttons, meta info
 └── Right: Video/photo with play overlay and glow effect
Section B: Digital Twin Console (chatbot)
 └── Full-width chat interface, max-width 4xl, centered
Section C: Problem Fit ("Where I'm Most Useful")

Section D: Bento Grid ("Operational Data")
 ├── Card 1 (4 cols): Career Trajectory — vertical timeline
 ├── Card 2 (4 cols): Skill Matrix — progress bars + tags
 └── Card 3 (4 cols): Impact Metrics — stat cards with numbers
Section E: Why AI CV
 └── Explanation paragraphs
Footer: CTA + contact buttons
VISUAL DESIGN RULES:
Background: Dark (zinc-950) with subtle texture overlay at 20% opacity
Glass panels: bg-white/5 backdrop-blur-xl border border-white/10 (the "glass-panel" class)
Accent color: Indigo-500/violet-500 gradient for CTAs, highlights, progress bars
Text hierarchy: white for headings, zinc-400 for body, zinc-500 for labels, indigo-400 for emphasis
Glow effects: Box shadows with rgba(79, 70, 229, 0.3) on key elements
Animations: Framer Motion — fade in from left for intro, stagger children for bento cards
Spacing: space-y-12 between major sections, generous padding (p-6 to p-8)

PART 3: THE CHATBOT — System Prompt Architecture
The chatbot quality is 80% system prompt design. Here is the exact methodology:
System Prompt Structure (in order):
1. IDENTITY & PERSONA (who you are)
2. RESPONSE STYLE SELECTION (how to classify question types)
3. SPECIAL TOPIC HANDLING (domains needing custom treatment)
4. WAR STORIES (specific examples to reference)
5. CRITICAL FORMATTING RULES
6. ANTI-REPETITION RULES
7. AFFIRMATIVE RESPONSE HANDLING
8. VOICE CALIBRATION (few-shot examples)
9. CORE PROFILE (career summary)
10. DETAILED WORK HISTORY (granular facts)
11. PHILOSOPHIES & APPROACH
12. CONTACT INFO
13. RESPONSE GUIDELINES
14. FORMATTING RULES (spacing, bullets, bold)
RESPONSE STYLE SELECTION — This is the most critical section:
The bot MUST classify every question into one of 4 types and respond differently:
Type 1: GENERAL/EXPLORATORY ("Tell me about your RPO work")
1-2 sentences of context
Key capabilities demonstrated
Scale/impact summary
End with: "Want me to walk through a specific project?"
NEVER use CAR format for these
Type 2: SPECIFIC PROJECT ("Walk me through how you built X")
Use Challenge / Approach / Result format
Each section on its own line with bold header
Include metrics in Result
Type 3: TRANSFERABLE SKILLS (topics they don't have direct experience in)
"While I haven't done [X] specifically, my experience in [related area] gives me transferable skills in..."
End with contact info
Type 4: OUTSIDE SCOPE (completely unrelated)
Brief redirect to their actual expertise
Contact info
WAR STORIES — How to Structure Them:
Each war story in the system prompt should have:
**Story Title (Company):**
Headline: "One-sentence hook with metric"
Depth: 3-4 sentences explaining what they actually did
Relevance: One sentence connecting to a broader principle
VOICE CALIBRATION — Include 3-5 Few-Shot Examples:
These are the MOST important part. They teach the AI the person's actual voice. Extract from questionnaire answers and rewrite as sample Q&A pairs:
**Example — Direct & Structural:**
User: "[Question]"
Response: "[Answer in their natural voice, with their vocabulary and sentence patterns]"
VOCABULARY GUIDANCE:
List 10 words they USE (extracted from their stories)
List 10 words to AVOID (generic corporate buzzwords)
Specify sentence structure: vary length, active voice, no "happy talk"
FORMATTING RULES (without these, responses look terrible):
1. Line breaks between paragraphs — NEVER walls of text
2. Bullet points for 3+ items (start with "- ")
3. **Bold** for key terms and pillar names
4. Short paragraphs — max 2-3 sentences
5. For CAR stories: each section gets its own bold header + paragraph
6. For lists/frameworks: use "- **Name** — description" format
ANTI-REPETITION + AFFIRMATIVE HANDLING:
- Don't repeat the same story twice in a conversation
- When user says "yes" / "sure" / "go ahead" — deliver on your previous offer immediately, no preamble
- Vary examples across different companies

PART 4: DIGITAL TWIN BRAIN — Client-Side Knowledge Base
This is the SECOND intelligence layer (alongside the Gemini system prompt). It provides fuzzy search + intent routing for the chat component.
Architecture:
TWIN_IDENTITY → persona definition
FACT_BANK → granular achievement bullets organized by company/role
KNOWLEDGE_BASE entries → structured stories with intent tags
detectIntent() → regex patterns mapping questions to intent categories
routeByPattern() → hard routes for common interview questions
Fuse.js fuzzy search → weighted search across all entry fields
Response ranking → type priority + intent matching + behavioral boost
How to Build from Questionnaire Data:
TWIN_IDENTITY: Map directly from Step 1 of questionnaire
FACT_BANK: Take Step 2 (career history bullets) and organize by company → role
KNOWLEDGE_BASE entries: Take Step 3 (CAR stories) and create one entry per story with:
id: kebab-case from story type + company (e.g., "failure-japan-pricing")
type: "experience" for CAR stories, "playbook" for approach/methodology stories
intent: ["behavioral"] for stories, ["scenario", "consulting"] for playbooks
keywords: 8-12 searchable terms extracted from the story
detectIntent(): Build regex patterns from the keyword clusters across all entries
routeByPattern(): Map common interview patterns ("tell me about a failure") to specific entry IDs

PART 5: KEY DESIGN DECISIONS THAT MAKE IT WORK
Data-driven, not hardcoded: Every piece of text comes from RESUME_DATA or the knowledge base. Components are pure renderers.
Two intelligence layers: Gemini handles natural conversation + context. The client-side brain provides deterministic routing for common questions. They work together — the brain feeds structured context to the Gemini prompt.
Streaming responses: SSE (Server-Sent Events) for real-time character-by-character response display. This feels much more natural than waiting for a complete response.
Conversation history: The full conversation is sent with each request so the AI maintains context and avoids repetition.
The intro message matters: It's the first thing people see. It should demonstrate the person's voice AND tell the user what to ask.
Suggested questions = showcase: The 8 suggested questions are carefully chosen to demonstrate RANGE — not just one dimension of the person.
Stats are proof: The Bento Grid impact metrics are the first thing a hiring manager scans. Every questionnaire answer should be mined for numbers.
Problem Fit flips the perspective: Instead of "I can do X" it says "You have problem X" — this is consultative framing and it's what makes the CV feel like a senior professional, not a job listing.
